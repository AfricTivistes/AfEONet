# **Civic Space Status:** Open




Liberia stands out as one of the strongest examples of an enabling civic space for citizen election observation in Africa, demonstrating how **post-conflict recovery can translate into institutionalized openness and trust**. The study places Liberia within the **open category across key dimensions**, reflecting a supportive legal framework, effective administrative systems, and strong collaboration between electoral authorities and civil society. 

At the regulatory level, Liberia provides a **clear and permissive legal environment**, with no significant restrictions on civil society engagement in election observation. Observer organizations are able to register, mobilize, and operate freely, reinforcing their independence and credibility. 

Administratively, Liberia performs strongly, with **transparent and predictable accreditation processes**, enabling timely deployment of observers across the electoral cycle. Citizen observer groups are granted access to polling stations and key electoral processes, facilitating comprehensive monitoring. 

The relationship between observer organizations and the National Elections Commission (NEC) is **highly collaborative and institutionalized**, with regular consultation, joint engagement in voter education, and structured post-election dialogue. Observer networks such as the Liberia Elections Observation Network (LEON) and other civil society coalitions play an active role in both **election monitoring and reform advocacy**, contributing to continuous improvement of electoral processes. 

In terms of **safety and operational conditions**, Liberia offers a relatively secure environment, with no widespread reports of intimidation or harassment of observers. This enables independent and nationwide deployment, strengthening the credibility of observation efforts. 

Access to election data is generally **available and supportive of transparency**, allowing observers to verify results and assess electoral integrity. Funding environments are also relatively open, with observer organizations able to mobilize resources from international partners without significant legal barriers. 

Public perception of citizen observers is **strongly positive**, with high levels of trust in their neutrality and contribution to democratic accountability. This legitimacy enhances both the effectiveness of observation and public confidence in electoral outcomes. 

**Implication:** Liberia demonstrates how post-conflict contexts can evolve into robust and enabling civic spaces through deliberate institutional development, sustained collaboration between electoral authorities and civil society, and the protection of observer independence.












## **Recommendations**

* **Sustain EMB–CSO collaboration frameworks**
*  **Expand observer roles into post-election reform processes**
*   **Strengthen domestic funding channels**
