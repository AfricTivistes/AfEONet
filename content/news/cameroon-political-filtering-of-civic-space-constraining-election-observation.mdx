# **Civic Space Status:** Obstructed / Repressed


Cameroon presents a civic space environment shaped by **selective enforcement of laws and politically influenced access to election observation**, placing it within the **obstructed to repressed categories** in the study. While legal provisions formally allow citizen election observation, their application is uneven and often favors organizations perceived as aligned with the state. 

At the regulatory and administrative levels, independent observer groups frequently face **delays in accreditation, restrictive licensing procedures, and opaque decision-making processes**, while pro-government or state-aligned organizations are able to operate with comparatively fewer constraints. This selective openness creates an uneven playing field that undermines the independence of civic oversight. 

The relationship between citizen observers and electoral authorities is **limited and often characterized by mistrust**, with minimal structured engagement or meaningful inclusion of independent voices in electoral processes. 

Security conditions further compound these challenges. In conflict-affected regions—particularly in the Anglophone areas—observers face **heightened risks, including intimidation, surveillance, and restricted mobility**, which significantly limit their ability to deploy and operate effectively. 

Access to election data is constrained, and funding environments remain sensitive, with regulatory pressures affecting the ability of independent organizations to mobilize and sustain observation efforts. Public perception of observers is also influenced by political narratives, contributing to skepticism toward independent monitoring initiatives. 

**Implication:** Cameroon demonstrates how selective openness and political filtering of civic space can weaken both the credibility and effectiveness of citizen election observation. Ensuring equal access, strengthening transparency, and improving security conditions will be critical to enabling independent and meaningful electoral oversight.










## **Recommendations**

* 
   **Ensure equal accreditation access for all observer groups**




* 
    **Reduce political interference in CSO operations**




* 
    **Strengthen security protections for observers in conflict regions**




* 
    **Enhance transparency in EMB decision-making processes**
