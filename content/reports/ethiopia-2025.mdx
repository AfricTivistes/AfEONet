# Ethiopia: Civic Space for Election Observers (2025)

**Composite Score:** 4.5/10 — **Repressed**

> Reforming but constraints persist

## Overview

Civic space is severely limited with active threats. Observers face hostile conditions and active interference.

Ethiopia received a composite score of **4.5/10** in the 2025 AfEONet assessment, placing it in the **Repressed** category. This assessment covers East Africa and evaluates eight dimensions of civic space for election observers.

## Analysis by Dimension

### 1. Regulatory Framework (5/10)

The legal and regulatory environment for election observers in Ethiopia offers partial protections with notable gaps.

### 2. Administrative Constraints (4/10)

Administrative processes create substantial barriers for observer organizations seeking accreditation and access.

### 3. EMB Relationship (5/10)

The relationship between civil society observer organizations and the Electoral Management Body is mixed, with areas of cooperation alongside tensions.

### 4. Security & Well-being (4/10)

Physical security conditions for election observers pose serious risks.

### 5. Access to Electoral Data (5/10)

Observer access to electoral data and transparency mechanisms exists but faces systematic limitations.

### 6. Access to Funding (4/10)

Financial sustainability for observer organizations is critically compromised.

### 7. Dialogue & Consultation (5/10)

Mechanisms for dialogue between observer organizations and government institutions exist but are inconsistent.

### 8. Perception of Observers (4/10)

Public and institutional perception of election observers is hostile, undermining observer credibility.

## Key Findings

- **Composite Score:** 4.5/10 (Repressed)
- **Strongest dimension:** Regulatory Framework (5/10)
- **Weakest dimension:** Administrative Constraints (4/10)
- **Region:** East Africa

## Recommendations

Based on the 2025 assessment, priority areas for improvement in Ethiopia include:

1. **Consolidate regulatory frameworks** — Close regulatory gaps affecting observer access
2. **Diversify funding mechanisms** — Reduce dependence on external donors through domestic resource mobilization
3. **Strengthen EMB-CSO collaboration** — Formalize cooperation protocols between EMBs and observer organizations
