# South Sudan: Civic Space for Election Observers (2025)

**Composite Score:** 3.5/10 — **Repressed**

> Fragility, hostile environment

## Overview

Civic space is severely limited with active threats. Observers face hostile conditions and active interference.

South Sudan received a composite score of **3.5/10** in the 2025 AfEONet assessment, placing it in the **Repressed** category. This assessment covers East Africa and evaluates eight dimensions of civic space for election observers.

## Analysis by Dimension

### 1. Regulatory Framework (3/10)

The legal and regulatory environment for election observers in South Sudan presents significant challenges.

### 2. Administrative Constraints (3/10)

Administrative processes create substantial barriers for observer organizations seeking accreditation and access.

### 3. EMB Relationship (4/10)

The relationship between civil society observer organizations and the Electoral Management Body is tense and adversarial.

### 4. Security & Well-being (3/10)

Physical security conditions for election observers pose serious risks.

### 5. Access to Electoral Data (4/10)

Observer access to electoral data and transparency mechanisms is severely restricted.

### 6. Access to Funding (3/10)

Financial sustainability for observer organizations is critically compromised.

### 7. Dialogue & Consultation (4/10)

Mechanisms for dialogue between observer organizations and government institutions are largely absent or performative.

### 8. Perception of Observers (3/10)

Public and institutional perception of election observers is hostile, undermining observer credibility.

## Key Findings

- **Composite Score:** 3.5/10 (Repressed)
- **Strongest dimension:** EMB Relationship (4/10)
- **Weakest dimension:** Regulatory Framework (3/10)
- **Region:** East Africa

## Recommendations

Based on the 2025 assessment, priority areas for improvement in South Sudan include:

1. **Reform regulatory frameworks** — Address restrictive laws limiting observer accreditation
2. **Diversify funding mechanisms** — Reduce dependence on external donors through domestic resource mobilization
3. **Rebuild EMB-CSO collaboration** — Re-establish basic dialogue channels with electoral authorities
