# Cameroon: Civic Space for Election Observers (2025)

**Composite Score:** 5.0/10 — **Obstructed**

> Political filtering of civic space, selective openness

## Overview

Civic space faces significant obstacles. Observers encounter systematic barriers to their work.

Cameroon received a composite score of **5.0/10** in the 2025 AfEONet assessment, placing it in the **Obstructed** category. This assessment covers Central Africa and evaluates eight dimensions of civic space for election observers.

## Analysis by Dimension

### 1. Regulatory Framework (5/10)

The legal and regulatory environment for election observers in Cameroon offers partial protections with notable gaps.

### 2. Administrative Constraints (4/10)

Administrative processes create substantial barriers for observer organizations seeking accreditation and access.

### 3. EMB Relationship (5/10)

The relationship between civil society observer organizations and the Electoral Management Body is mixed, with areas of cooperation alongside tensions.

### 4. Security & Well-being (4/10)

Physical security conditions for election observers pose serious risks.

### 5. Access to Electoral Data (5/10)

Observer access to electoral data and transparency mechanisms exists but faces systematic limitations.

### 6. Access to Funding (5/10)

Financial sustainability for observer organizations is precarious, with heavy dependence on external sources.

### 7. Dialogue & Consultation (5/10)

Mechanisms for dialogue between observer organizations and government institutions exist but are inconsistent.

### 8. Perception of Observers (5/10)

Public and institutional perception of election observers is mixed, with some acceptance and some suspicion.

## Key Findings

- **Composite Score:** 5.0/10 (Obstructed)
- **Strongest dimension:** Regulatory Framework (5/10)
- **Weakest dimension:** Administrative Constraints (4/10)
- **Region:** Central Africa

## Recommendations

Based on the 2025 assessment, priority areas for improvement in Cameroon include:

1. **Consolidate regulatory frameworks** — Close regulatory gaps affecting observer access
2. **Develop funding mechanisms** — Build more sustainable and diversified funding models
3. **Strengthen EMB-CSO collaboration** — Formalize cooperation protocols between EMBs and observer organizations
