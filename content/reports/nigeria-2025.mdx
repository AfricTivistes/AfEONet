# Nigeria: Civic Space for Election Observers (2025)

**Composite Score:** 5.5/10 — **Obstructed**

> IReV present but limited funding and digital transparency

## Overview

Civic space faces significant obstacles. Observers encounter systematic barriers to their work.

Nigeria received a composite score of **5.5/10** in the 2025 AfEONet assessment, placing it in the **Obstructed** category. This assessment covers West Africa and evaluates eight dimensions of civic space for election observers.

## Analysis by Dimension

### 1. Regulatory Framework (6/10)

The legal and regulatory environment for election observers in Nigeria offers partial protections with notable gaps.

### 2. Administrative Constraints (5/10)

Administrative processes present manageable but notable obstacles for observer organizations seeking accreditation and access.

### 3. EMB Relationship (6/10)

The relationship between civil society observer organizations and the Electoral Management Body is mixed, with areas of cooperation alongside tensions.

### 4. Security & Well-being (5/10)

Physical security conditions for election observers present moderate concerns requiring vigilance.

### 5. Access to Electoral Data (7/10)

Observer access to electoral data and transparency mechanisms is strong and institutionalized.

### 6. Access to Funding (4/10)

Financial sustainability for observer organizations is critically compromised.

### 7. Dialogue & Consultation (6/10)

Mechanisms for dialogue between observer organizations and government institutions exist but are inconsistent.

### 8. Perception of Observers (5/10)

Public and institutional perception of election observers is mixed, with some acceptance and some suspicion.

## Key Findings

- **Composite Score:** 5.5/10 (Obstructed)
- **Strongest dimension:** Access to Electoral Data (7/10)
- **Weakest dimension:** Access to Funding (4/10)
- **Region:** West Africa

## Recommendations

Based on the 2025 assessment, priority areas for improvement in Nigeria include:

1. **Consolidate regulatory frameworks** — Close regulatory gaps affecting observer access
2. **Diversify funding mechanisms** — Reduce dependence on external donors through domestic resource mobilization
3. **Strengthen EMB-CSO collaboration** — Formalize cooperation protocols between EMBs and observer organizations
