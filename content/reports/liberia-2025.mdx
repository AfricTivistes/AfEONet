# Liberia: Civic Space for Election Observers (2025)

**Composite Score:** 7.0/10 — **Restricted**

> Post-conflict constraints shaping civic space

## Overview

Civic space faces some limitations but remains largely functional. Observers can operate with manageable constraints.

Liberia received a composite score of **7.0/10** in the 2025 AfEONet assessment, placing it in the **Restricted** category. This assessment covers West Africa and evaluates eight dimensions of civic space for election observers.

## Analysis by Dimension

### 1. Regulatory Framework (7/10)

The legal and regulatory environment for election observers in Liberia provides solid foundations for civic engagement.

### 2. Administrative Constraints (7/10)

Administrative processes function effectively for observer organizations seeking accreditation and access.

### 3. EMB Relationship (7/10)

The relationship between civil society observer organizations and the Electoral Management Body is characterized by strong collaboration and mutual respect.

### 4. Security & Well-being (6/10)

Physical security conditions for election observers present moderate concerns requiring vigilance.

### 5. Access to Electoral Data (7/10)

Observer access to electoral data and transparency mechanisms is strong and institutionalized.

### 6. Access to Funding (6/10)

Financial sustainability for observer organizations is precarious, with heavy dependence on external sources.

### 7. Dialogue & Consultation (7/10)

Mechanisms for dialogue between observer organizations and government institutions are functional and inclusive.

### 8. Perception of Observers (7/10)

Public and institutional perception of election observers is largely positive, supporting their mandate.

## Key Findings

- **Composite Score:** 7.0/10 (Restricted)
- **Strongest dimension:** Regulatory Framework (7/10)
- **Weakest dimension:** Security & Well-being (6/10)
- **Region:** West Africa

## Recommendations

Based on the 2025 assessment, priority areas for improvement in Liberia include:

1. **Sustain and strengthen regulatory frameworks** — Maintain the enabling legal environment
2. **Develop funding mechanisms** — Build more sustainable and diversified funding models
3. **Deepen EMB-CSO collaboration** — Institutionalize joint working mechanisms
