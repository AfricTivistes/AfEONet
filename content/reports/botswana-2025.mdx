# Botswana: Civic Space for Election Observers (2025)

**Composite Score:** 9.0/10 — **Open**

> Open/Free environment, transparent processes

## Overview

Civic space is fully respected and protected. Election observers operate freely with strong institutional support.

Botswana received a composite score of **9.0/10** in the 2025 AfEONet assessment, placing it in the **Open** category. This assessment covers Southern Africa and evaluates eight dimensions of civic space for election observers.

## Analysis by Dimension

### 1. Regulatory Framework (9/10)

The legal and regulatory environment for election observers in Botswana provides solid foundations for civic engagement.

### 2. Administrative Constraints (9/10)

Administrative processes function effectively for observer organizations seeking accreditation and access.

### 3. EMB Relationship (9/10)

The relationship between civil society observer organizations and the Electoral Management Body is characterized by strong collaboration and mutual respect.

### 4. Security & Well-being (9/10)

Physical security conditions for election observers are generally favorable.

### 5. Access to Electoral Data (9/10)

Observer access to electoral data and transparency mechanisms is strong and institutionalized.

### 6. Access to Funding (8/10)

Financial sustainability for observer organizations is relatively secure.

### 7. Dialogue & Consultation (9/10)

Mechanisms for dialogue between observer organizations and government institutions are functional and inclusive.

### 8. Perception of Observers (9/10)

Public and institutional perception of election observers is largely positive, supporting their mandate.

## Key Findings

- **Composite Score:** 9.0/10 (Open)
- **Strongest dimension:** Regulatory Framework (9/10)
- **Weakest dimension:** Access to Funding (8/10)
- **Region:** Southern Africa

## Recommendations

Based on the 2025 assessment, priority areas for improvement in Botswana include:

1. **Sustain and strengthen regulatory frameworks** — Maintain the enabling legal environment
2. **Maintain funding mechanisms** — Ensure sustainable domestic financing
3. **Deepen EMB-CSO collaboration** — Institutionalize joint working mechanisms
